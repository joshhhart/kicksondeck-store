Every Yeezy 350 V2 Colorway, Explained (2026 Visual Guide)

Kicks on Deck · June 24, 2026 · 7 min read

The Yeezy Boost 350 V2 is the most recognizable silhouette of the last decade — and also the most confusing, because there are dozens of colorways and the names rarely tell you what the shoe actually looks like. This is the map.

The icons everyone knows

A handful of colorways did the heavy lifting for the 350 V2's fame:

  • Zebra — white Primeknit with the bold red "SPLY-350" stripe. The single most photographed 350 V2 ever. If you own one pair, most people start here.
  • Beluga 2.0 — grey upper with the orange stripe. The OG hype shoe, softened into an everyday grey for 2.0.
  • Bred — triple black with a red stripe. The stealth flex.

The quiet ones that age best

The loud pairs get the likes, but the muted runs get worn the most:

  • Cream / Triple White — all-white, no stripe. Goes with everything, scuffs are part of the charm.
  • Bone, Sand Taupe, Natural — earth tones that disappear into any fit. These are the "I just throw them on" pairs.

Reflective vs non-reflective

Several colorways come in a reflective version, where the threads in the upper light up under a camera flash or headlights. The Static Reflective is the cleanest example — daylight clean, nighttime loud. If you shoot a lot of content or just like a hidden detail, the reflective tax is worth it.

Mono runs

The Mono series (Mono Ice, Mono Clay, Mono Cinder) swaps the woven Primeknit for a single-tone molded mesh. They look more like a slip-on runner and lean futuristic. Polarizing, but they've quietly become some of the most comfortable to wear.

So which one should you get?

A simple rule:

Your first 350 V2 should be a neutral (Cream, Bone, or Beluga). Your second should be the loud one you actually wanted (Zebra, Bred, or a reflective).
