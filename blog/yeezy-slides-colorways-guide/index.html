Every Yeezy Slide Colorway, Explained (2026 Guide)

Kicks on Deck · July 4, 2026 · 6 min read

The Yeezy Slide lineup has quietly gotten as deep as the 350 V2's — same EVA foam mold, a dozen-plus colorways, and names that don't tell you much on their own. Here's the map.

The everyday neutrals

These are the pairs that never leave the rotation because they go with anything:

  • Onyx — matte black on black. The one pair that works with every fit, no exceptions.
  • Bone — warm off-white, including the 2022/2023 restock run. Scuffs blend in instead of standing out.
  • Pure — the lightest, cleanest neutral in the lineup. Reads almost grey depending on the light.
  • Desert Sand, Flax, Earth Brown — three shades of the same idea: warm, muted, built to disappear into a fit rather than lead it.

The ones that actually stand out

For when the slide is the point:

  • Enflame Orange — the loudest colorway in the current lineup. Built for pool days and content, not stealth.
  • Glow Green (original and 2022/2023 restock) — a genuine glow-in-the-dark run. The kind of detail that only shows up in the dark, which is exactly why it's a favorite for collectors.
  • Granite, Resin, Core — cooler-toned pairs with more contrast than the neutrals, without going full loud.
  • Azure, Soot, Ochre — the rotating middle ground: enough color to notice, still easy to style.

Restocks vs original runs

A few colorways (Bone, Glow Green) have both an original release and a 2022/2023 restock version. The mold and comfort are identical — the restocks just reflect the more recent production run, which is what we currently carry in most sizes.

How to actually wear them

Slides live in three modes: poolside (any colorway works), socks-and-slides streetwear (neutrals like Onyx or Bone read cleanest), and travel/airport (Bone or Pure so they don't clash with whatever you packed). If you already own a 350 V2 or Foam RNNR in a neutral tone, match your slide to it and you've got a rotation that works together instead of fighting for attention.

Slides are the lowest-commitment way to try the Yeezy foam feel. If you're on the fence about Foam RNNRs, start here first.
