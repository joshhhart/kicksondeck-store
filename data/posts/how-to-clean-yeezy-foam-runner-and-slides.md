Foam Runners and Slides are one-piece EVA foam — no knit, no stitching, no boost sole. That's exactly why people baby the wrong parts of the cleaning process and skip the parts that matter. Dirt sits *in* the textured surface, not on top of it, and the wrong cleaner leaves grey blotches that never fully lift. Here's the method that actually works.

## What you actually need

- Lukewarm water, never hot
- A soft-bristle brush — the textured tread on both silhouettes grabs dirt, so a [dedicated sneaker brush](/product/shoe-brush-with-automatic-liquid-dispenser/) gets into the ridges better than a rag
- A few drops of mild soap or dish soap — no bleach, no magic eraser
- A microfiber towel

## The method

1. **Rinse loose debris first.** Foam Runners especially collect dirt in the perforated holes and tread — a quick rinse under the tap knocks most of it out before you scrub anything.
2. **Light circles, not pressure.** EVA foam is soft and scratches or compresses if you dig in. Dip the brush, work small circles, let the soap lift the grime instead of muscling it out.
3. **Get the tread and straps.** On [Slides](/collection/slides/), the strap and footbed groove trap the most dirt. On [Foam Runners](/collection/foam-rnnr/), it's the perforations and outsole tread — same light-pressure approach.
4. **Wipe fully dry.** Foam holds water longer than knit. Towel off every surface, including inside the perforations, so it doesn't sit damp.
5. **Air dry flat, out of the sun.** Direct heat and UV yellow and stiffen EVA foam the same way they do a boost sole — never a radiator, never a car dashboard.

## What actually stains foam (and what doesn't)

Grass and mud are the two repeat offenders — they bind to EVA foam almost like a dye rather than sitting on the surface. Get to them same-day with soap and water; let them dry in and a light grey ghost stain is permanent. Bleach doesn't help here either — it discolors foam unevenly instead of lifting stains clean, which is the opposite of what people expect.

> Never: bleach, magic erasers, or the washing machine. All three either discolor or warp EVA foam permanently.

## Keep them looking new

A 30-second rinse-and-brush after any wear near grass, sand, or mud keeps stains from setting. Store both silhouettes flat rather than crammed in a bag — folded foam creases in a way that doesn't bounce back the way knit does.
